Blender Hollandaise Sauce

Description

Hollandaise sauce is a classic French sauce that is commonly served with breakfast and brunch dishes such as eggs Benedict or asparagus. It is a rich and creamy sauce made from egg yolks, butter, lemon juice, and a touch of cayenne pepper. The sauce is prepared using a double boiler method, which involves whisking the egg yolks and lemon juice together over a pot of simmering water until they thicken. Melted butter is then gradually added to the mixture while whisking constantly, resulting in a smooth and velvety sauce with a tangy flavor and a buttery texture.

Hollandaise sauce is a versatile condiment that can be used in a variety of ways. It can be served hot or cold, and is often used as a topping for steamed vegetables such as asparagus or broccoli. It can also be used as a dipping sauce for seafood or as a dressing for salads. While it can be a bit tricky to prepare, the end result is a decadent and flavorful sauce that is sure to impress. Whether served at a fancy brunch or as a special treat for a weeknight dinner, Hollandaise sauce is a delicious addition to any meal.

Ingredients

  • Egg yolks: This creamy Hollandaise recipe starts with three egg yolks.
  • Lemon juice: A tablespoon of lemon juice adds lots of freshness and brightness..
  • Dijon mustard:
    • Dijon mustard lends a sharp and tangy flavor.
  • Hot sauce:
    • Use a dash (or more) of your favorite hot pepper sauce.
  • Butter:
    • Rich, velvety Hollandaise requires a whole stick of butter.
